Why Edge

What changes when nothing leaves the device.

Most computer vision products send frames to a server for inference. SensorDyme doesn't have that option to begin with — the model runs where the camera is.

Technology

Built edge-first, not cloud-first.

SensorDyme runs entirely on Raspberry Pi 5 class hardware — no GPU, no accelerator card, no dedicated inference server. Every model executes on-CPU, at the edge, in real time.

Perception is handled by a combination of MediaPipe for landmark and pose tracking, and YOLO-nano for lightweight object detection — models chosen specifically because they run efficiently without specialized hardware.

The result is a device that processes 25–30 frames per second locally, with no dependency on network bandwidth, latency, or uptime to function.

Process

From box to first alert.

Deploying a new module doesn't mean a new install. Most sites go from unboxing to live events in under an hour.

1

Mount the device

Attach the camera at the height and angle appropriate for the task — a vehicle dash, a doorway, a warehouse aisle.

2

Choose your module

Select the perception task — attention, occupancy, pose, PPE, or counting. No new hardware to order.

3

Inference runs on-device

The model processes frames locally at 25–30 FPS. Nothing is uploaded, recorded, or queued for review.

4

Review events, not footage

Scores, counts, and alerts reach you in real time — the frames that produced them never do.

Privacy

Privacy isn't a policy. It's the architecture.

SensorDyme is designed so that privacy is a property of how the system is built, not a promise layered on top of it.

On-device processing

Every frame is captured, analyzed, and discarded on the device itself. Inference never depends on a round trip to a server, in a classroom, a vehicle, or a warehouse floor.

Landmarks, not video

Models output numerical landmarks, poses, and counts — not images. There is no video to review, export, or misplace, regardless of which module is running.

No cloud dependency

The device operates fully offline. Connectivity is optional, used only to deliver the resulting events, not the source frames, even at multi-site scale.
